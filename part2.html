PART II

Measuring Traffic Congestion

This part focuses on how congestion is measured from the relationship between traffic pressure and road supply, and how the resulting congestion patterns are interpreted through typology and weekday–weekend transitions.

ROAD SUPPLY

Road Supply Index (RSI)

Road supply through network density and effective area

Key components
Road network classified into:
motorway · trunk · primary · secondary · tertiary · other

Two complementary dimensions of supply:
RLD – Road Length Density
RAD – Road Area Density

1. Buffer by road class (e.g. 30–5 m)
2. Compute per-grid:

RLDraw = Σk wk · ldk      RADraw = Σk wk · adk

3. Robust normalization:

RSI = (RLD* + RAD*) / 2
(* indicates robust normalization)

CONGESTION INDEX

Congestion Index: Balancing Pressure and Supply

Definition:

CI = VTI* − RSI*
* indicates ECDF normalization

CI > 0
→ Observed traffic pressure exceeds local road supply

CI < 0
→ Road supply is sufficient for observed traffic

Key Processing Steps
Robust scaling applied before comparison
Quantile / ECDF mapping used to:

  • mitigate scale mismatch between demand & supply
  • handle saturation in street-level observations

CI computed consistently across all hours and day types
