PART I

Traffic Distribution

Mode-specific vehicle intensity across space (250m grid, street-view detections). Use the toggles to compare Motorcycle, Car, and Bus across three metrics.

What this part shows

We start from street-view image detections (motorcycles, cars, buses), then aggregate them into a consistent 250m × 250m grid to enable citywide comparison. The same grid is visualized through three lenses: (1) Detection Count (total exposure), (2) Grid Mean Intensity (average intensity), and (3) Mode Share (relative composition within each grid cell).

1) Detection Count (n)

Point-level detections aggregated to grid (all observations).

How to read

  • Count (n) summarizes total detected vehicles aggregated into each 250m grid cell.
  • Higher values indicate stronger street-level exposure captured by SVI samples.
  • Use the toggle to compare Motorcycle / Car / Bus under the same spatial unit.

What it implies

  • This is a volume-like indicator influenced by both traffic presence and SVI coverage.
  • It is useful for identifying where each mode concentrates at city scale.
  • We interpret it together with mean and share to reduce sampling bias.
COUNT · MOTORCYCLE
Field: n_motorcycle
Data source: SVI object detection → grid aggregation. Boundary overlays: HCMC boundary (red), wards/districts (black).

2) Grid Mean Intensity

All-time mean per grid (mode-specific).

Method (PPT Step 1 → Step 2)

  • Step 1: compute hourly grid mean by averaging detections within the same hour and grid.
  • Step 2: average across all observed time slices to get an all-time mean per grid.
  • This focuses on typical intensity rather than simply “how many images we have”.

Interpretation

  • Mean intensity highlights persistent hotspots rather than one-off spikes.
  • It enables fairer comparison when SVI sampling density varies.
  • Compare modes to see where the city is motorcycle-dominant, car-heavy, or bus-present.
MEAN · MOTORCYCLE
Field: n_motorcycle_mean_all
Mean bins follow your PPT legend ranges (fixed breaks, mode-specific).

3) Mode Composition (Share)

Observed mode share per grid (relative composition).

Definition

  • Share represents the relative composition of modes within each grid cell.
  • Computed as mode mean / total mean (motorcycle + car + bus) per grid.
  • It describes street-level exposure structure, not a travel-survey modal split.

Why it matters

  • Share separates “busy areas” from “mode-dominant areas”.
  • It reveals whether corridors are car-oriented or motorcycle baseline.
  • Even small bus share clusters can indicate public transport influence.

Field: share_motorcycle_all
Share bins follow your PPT legend ranges (fixed breaks, mode-specific).

Takeaway from Part I

Together, count (total exposure), mean (typical intensity), and share (relative composition) provide a layered picture of where each mode concentrates and how street-level mobility structure varies across HCMC. In Part II, we translate these patterns into congestion indicators across space and time.
