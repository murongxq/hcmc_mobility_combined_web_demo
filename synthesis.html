DATA AND ANALYSIS STRUCTURE

From Data Collection to Analytical Outputs

1. Street-View Observation Data

Data Source

Large-scale street-view imagery collected through the Mapillary platform, providing geolocated images of urban streets with timestamps and spatial coordinates.

Processing

AI-based object detection was applied to identify visible transport modes within each image, including:

  • motorcycles
  • cars
  • buses

This enables extraction of mode-specific traffic observations at the street level.

Analytical Outputs
  • Traffic distribution intensity across the city
  • Mode composition comparison
  • Spatial clustering of traffic composition
  • Grid-based aggregation of observed traffic intensity
Role in the framework

Provides the demand-side representation of mobility, capturing how different transport modes are actually used across urban space.

2. Road Network Structure

Data Source

Open road network datasets describing the physical road hierarchy:

  • motorway
  • trunk
  • primary
  • secondary
  • tertiary
  • other streets
Derived Indicators

Road network geometry was used to compute several structural indicators:

  • Road Length Density (RLD)
  • Road Area Density (RAD)
  • Road Supply Index (RSI)

These indicators represent the infrastructure capacity available for traffic flow.

Analytical Outputs
  • Spatial variation of road supply capacity
  • Structural road hierarchy patterns
  • Baseline supply conditions for congestion evaluation
Role in the framework

Represents the infrastructure supply side of the mobility system.

3. Public Transport Data

Data Source

Public transport datasets collected from BusMap Vietnam, including:

  • bus routes
  • stops
  • schedule information
  • vehicle capacity
  • fare information
Processing

Using schedule data, bus movements were simulated across the network over time, allowing estimation of:

  • bus frequency
  • vehicle presence
  • seat availability
Analytical Outputs
  • Hourly public transport supply
  • Seat supply per grid cell
  • Accessibility within 30-minute and 60-minute travel thresholds
  • Reachable stops analysis through an interactive tool
Role in the framework

Represents the planned transport supply system, allowing comparison between infrastructure provision and observed travel behavior.

4. Unified Spatial and Temporal Framework

Spatial Unit

250 m × 250 m grid cells covering the entire metropolitan area.

Temporal Unit

Hourly time steps from 06:00 to 23:00, with separate aggregation for:

  • weekday conditions
  • weekend conditions
Purpose

This unified framework allows datasets derived from different sources to be directly comparable across space and time.

Role in the framework

Acts as the integration layer connecting traffic observations, congestion measures, and transport supply.

5. Integrated Comparative Indicators

Indicators
  • Bus Supply → seat_sum
  • Motorcycle Intensity → n_motorcycle
  • Congestion Level → CI_final
  • Bus Share → share_bus_all

All indicators were aggregated at the 250 m grid level.

Method

A median split classification was applied:

  • High: value greater than the city median
  • Low: value equal to or below the city median

This produces quadrant-based comparisons between variables.

Analytical Outputs
  • Public Transport Supply × Motorcycle Distribution
  • Public Transport Supply × Bus Share
  • Public Transport Supply × Congestion Index
Role in the framework

Reveals supply–demand mismatches, modal substitution patterns, and congestion pressure zones.

6. Cluster-Based Urban Typology

Method

K-Means clustering using combined variables of:

  • public transport supply
  • motorcycle intensity
  • congestion index
Outputs
  • Motorcycle-Dependent Areas — High motorcycle usage and congestion
  • Transit-Strained Core — High public transport presence but still congested
  • Car-Oriented Districts — Higher private vehicle dominance
  • Transit-Effective Zones — High public transport supply with relatively low motorcycle dependence
Role in the framework

Transforms multiple analytical layers into interpretable urban mobility patterns.

Overall Analytical Workflow

The framework connects multiple heterogeneous datasets — street-level observations, road network structure, and public transport schedules — into a unified spatial grid system. This integration enables comparative analysis of mobility demand, congestion pressure, and transport supply across Ho Chi Minh City, while the micro-level tools provide on-site evidence to interpret how these macro patterns are actually experienced.
