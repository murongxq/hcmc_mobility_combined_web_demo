Motivation

Research motivation & core question

Key motivation

Mobility in Ho Chi Minh City is dominated by motorcycles, while public transport remains limited but potentially critical. However, high-resolution, mode-specific traffic datasets are often unavailable or inconsistent, making it difficult to compare modes fairly across space and time.

We therefore build a workflow that connects street-view object detection, road network structure, and schedule-based public transport supply, and then interpret them under one unified framework.

  • Limited availability of high-resolution, mode-specific traffic data
  • Existing studies often overlook temporal dynamics and modal differences
  • Public transport plays a limited but potentially critical role

What this project aims to deliver

The goal is not to claim exact “ground truth” traffic counts, but to build a comparable view of mobility dynamics: distribution (where), congestion (how intense), and supply (when and how much).
