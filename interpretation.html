INTERPRETATION

Integrated Interpretation

Integrating distribution, congestion, and public transport through a common 250m grid framework. This section cross-reads bus supply, motorcycle intensity, bus street visibility, and congestion index to identify mismatches, benchmark areas, and structurally stressed corridors.

Integrating Accessibility, Supply, Demand & Congestion

Data Foundation

• Bus Supply → seat_sum

• Motorcycle Intensity → n_motorcycle

• Congestion Index → CI_final

• Bus Share → share_bus_all

All indicators aggregated at 250m grid level.

Method

Median split for each indicator

High: value > city median

Low: value ≤ city median

Quadrant-based classification

Three Comparative Analyses

Public Transport Supply × Motorcycle Distribution

• Identify spatial supply–demand mismatch

• Detect under-served motorcycle-dependent areas

Public Transport Supply × Bus Share

• Examine effectiveness of bus provision

• Evaluate modal substitution performance

Public Transport Supply × Congestion Index

• Assess whether supply alleviates congestion

• Identify structurally stressed corridors

Public Transport Supply vs Motorcycle Distribution

Q1 – High Supply / High Motorcycle → Supply exists but modal shift not achieved → Public transport not attractive enough → Possible behavioral or network barrier
Q2 – Low Supply / High Motorcycle → Clear underserved area → Strong dependence on motorcycles → Priority zone for supply expansion
Q3 – Low Supply / Low Motorcycle → Possibly low demand or peripheral area → Infrastructure-light zones
Q4 – High Supply / Low Motorcycle → Successful modal substitution → Potential benchmark areas

Public Transport Supply vs Bus Share (Street Visibility)

Q1 – High Supply / High Bus Share → Strong public transport presence → High network integration → Transit-oriented pattern
Q2 – Low Supply / High Bus Share → Visual dominance but limited capacity → Possibly corridor concentration
Q3 – Low Supply / Low Bus Share → Public transport marginal → Car/motorcycle dominant streets
Q4 – High Supply / Low Bus Share → Invisible supply → Buses not spatially embedded in street fabric

Public Transport Supply vs Congestion Index

Q1 – High Supply / High Congestion → Supply insufficient relative to demand → Structural bottleneck → Need network redesign
Q2 – Low Supply / High Congestion → Under-served congested zone → Priority intervention area
Q3 – Low Supply / Low Congestion → Low activity / low pressure zone
Q4 – High Supply / Low Congestion → Effective transit support → Potential model corridor
