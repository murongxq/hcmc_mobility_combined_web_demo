MOTIVATION

Why HCMC needs a system transition

Core challenge

  • Motorcycles remain the dominant daily travel mode, accounting for about 74% of trips.
  • Metro Line 1 creates a transition opportunity, but localized improvements are not enough to reshape the whole system.
  • The central question is how metro development, smart mobility planning, and data-driven governance can support a realistic modal transition.

CHAPTERS

Follow the project workflow

start with the analytical framework →
Framework
A unified 250m grid and time-based framework linking traffic distribution, congestion, and public transport supply.
Part I — Traffic Distribution
Street-view object detection is used to map motorcycle, car, and bus presence across the 250m grid.
Part II — Measuring Congestion
A grid-based congestion index compares observed traffic pressure with road supply across space and time.
Part III — Public Transport Supply
Bus network data, schedules, capacity, and reachability are used to evaluate public transport supply in space and time.
Interpretation
Traffic composition, congestion mechanisms, and mobility clusters are combined to identify spatial priorities.
Synthesis
Data-driven results are linked with Line 1 and Line 2 field observations to explain behavior and corridor-level system limits.
Mobility Analyzer
An interactive smart mobility tool for grid-level diagnosis, AI-assisted strategy selection, street-view prompts, and future platform functions.
Conclusion
Final answer to the project question: how HCMC can move from metro construction toward coordinated, data-driven system transition.
