PART III

Public Transport Supply

This part reconstructs public transport supply from schedule data, then compares how supply varies across space (250m grid) and time (hourly, weekday vs weekend). It also introduces a network-based accessibility lens (30/60 min reachable stops).

Building the HCMC Bus Network

Data source
BusMap Vietnam (public transport app)

Collected information

  • Bus routes (geometry)
  • Stop locations
  • Schedules (time of trip, headway)
  • Vehicle capacity
  • Fare information

Output

  • Full city-scale bus network
  • Structured stop–route–schedule dataset

Public Transport Supply Analysis (Weekday vs Weekend)

We aggregate simulated vehicle positions to a 250m grid and compute two metrics: mean bus count per minute and mean seat count per minute.

Weekday pattern

  • 6–9 AM → Morning peak
  • 9–12 → Clear drop
  • 12–1 → Short rebound
  • 1–5 → Lower steady period
  • 5–7 → Evening peak
  • After 7 → Decline

Weekend pattern

  • 9 AM → Rising
  • 9–12 → High level
  • 1–3 → Slight drop
  • 3–8 → Sustained high
  • After 8 → Gradual decline

Network-Based Accessibility (Grid Level)

Method overview
Each grid cell measures: number of bus stops that can reach it.

Travel model assumptions

  • Dwell time: 20 sec
  • Waiting time: headway / 2
  • Route-specific speed (time of trip based)
  • Transfer radius: 150 m
  • Walking speed: 1.3 m/s
  • 30 min and 60 min computed independently

Output
Grid-level accessibility intensity (30 min / 60 min).

Accessibility (30 min)

Field: bus_access_score_30min
Higher value → more stops can reach the grid within 30 minutes.

Accessibility (60 min)

Field: bus_access_score_60min
Higher value → more stops can reach the grid within 60 minutes.
