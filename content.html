STRUCTURE

From Analysis to System Understanding

This project is structured as a multi-layer mobility analysis, progressing from data-driven pattern identification to system-level interpretation, and further into interactive exploration and planning application.

Macro-Level Mobility Analysis

The macro analysis investigates how mobility patterns emerge from the interaction between traffic distribution, congestion dynamics, and transport supply, using a unified 250m grid + time framework.

1. Research Motivation & Core Question
Mobility is often treated as an infrastructure problem. This study reframes it as a system interaction problem, highlighting the need for integrated analysis across modes and time.
2. Analytical Framework
A unified spatiotemporal framework linking distribution, congestion, and supply, enabling cross-dimensional interpretation at the city scale.
3. Traffic Distribution
Mode-specific spatial patterns derived from street-view detection, revealing the dominance and variation of different transport modes.
4. Congestion Measurement
Spatiotemporal congestion indicators capturing dynamic pressure across the city, highlighting mismatches between demand and infrastructure.
5. Public Transport Supply
Schedule-based bus simulation and accessibility analysis, evaluating how supply interacts with existing mobility demand.
6. Interpretation
Integrated reading across datasets to identify spatial typologies, priority zones, and underlying system mechanisms.
7. Synthesis
Linking macro patterns with micro observations to form a coherent understanding of mobility challenges and opportunities.
8. Mobility Analyzer
An interactive diagnostic tool that connects spatial indicators, congestion types, and AI-assisted planning insights.
9. Conclusion
Final synthesis of findings, strategic implications, and directions for future mobility transformation.

Micro-Level Investigation

The micro-level study complements macro analysis by examining real-world transport experience and corridor conditions, providing qualitative validation of system-level findings.

Public Transport Experience
On-site observation of bus and metro usage, including waiting, transfers, and passenger behavior, revealing operational realities beyond data.
Metro & Corridor Context
Analysis of metro development and surrounding street conditions, highlighting integration challenges and spatial constraints.
