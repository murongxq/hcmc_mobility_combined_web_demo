PART I

Traffic Distribution

Mode-specific vehicle intensity across space. We begin with street-view detections of motorcycles, cars, and buses, then compare them through three grid-based readings: detection count, all-time mean traffic intensity, and observed mode share. Finally, we translate relative traffic composition into a typology of motorcycle baseline, car-oriented, bus-influenced, mixed, and low confidence.

What this part shows

We start from street-view image detections (motorcycles, cars, buses), then aggregate them into a consistent 250m × 250m grid to enable citywide comparison. The same grid is visualized through three lenses: (1) Detection Count for total observed exposure, (2) Grid Mean Intensity for typical mode-specific intensity, and (3) Mode Share for relative composition within each grid cell. The last step further summarizes this composition into a traffic composition typology based on deviation from the citywide baseline.

2) Grid-Based Mean Traffic Intensity

Grid-Level Average Traffic Intensity (All-Time Mean). This second step moves from total detections to typical intensity in each grid.

Step 1 — Hourly Grid Mean

Ig,h,d(m) = (1 / Ng,h,d) × Σi ∈ (g,h,d) ni(m)
where: g = grid, h = hour, d = day type, m = mode, and ni(m) = detected count per image

Step 2 — All-Time Mean per Grid

Īg(m) = (1 / Tg) × Σh,d Ig,h,d(m)
where Tg = number of observed time slices in grid g

Interpretation

  • Mean intensity highlights persistent hotspots rather than one-off spikes.
  • It enables fairer comparison when SVI sampling density varies.
  • Comparing modes shows where the city is more motorcycle-dominant, car-heavy, or bus-present.
MEAN · MOTORCYCLE
Field: n_motorcycle_mean_all
Mean bins follow your PPT legend ranges, so the webpage and slides use the same fixed breaks for mode-specific interpretation.

3) Mode Composition (Observed Share)

Observed Mode Share by Grid. This layer focuses on relative composition rather than absolute intensity.

Definition

Sg(m) = Īg(m) / Σk Īg(k)
where k ∈ {motorcycle, car, bus}

Share = mode mean / total mean

What it means

  • Relative composition within each grid
  • Represents street-level exposure structure
  • Not equivalent to travel survey modal split
SHARE · MOTORCYCLE
Field: share_motorcycle_all
Share separates “busy places” from “mode-dominant places”. Even where total traffic is not highest, a high local share can still reveal strong car orientation or visible bus influence.

4) Traffic Composition Typology

Traffic Composition Types (Top 20% Deviation Classification). This final map translates local composition into interpretable traffic types.

Step 1 — Baseline

S̄(m) = (1 / G) × Σg Sg(m)

Step 2 — Deviation

Δg(m) = Sg(m) − S̄(m)

Step 3 — Classification (Top 20%)

Δg(m) ≥ Q0.8(Δ(m))
g = grid cell (250m × 250m), m = traffic mode (motorcycle, car, bus), Sg(m) = observed mode share in grid g, S̄(m) = city-level baseline share, Δg(m) = deviation from baseline, Q0.8 = 80th percentile threshold

Classification based on deviation from city baseline

  • Motorcycle Baseline
  • Car-Oriented
  • Bus-Influenced
  • Mixed
  • Low Confidence

This typology does not measure total magnitude directly. Instead, it shows which grids deviate most strongly from the citywide composition baseline.

Takeaway from Part I

Together, count, mean intensity, observed share, and traffic composition typology provide a layered picture of where each mode concentrates and how street-level mobility structure varies across HCMC. Part II then builds on these spatial patterns to measure congestion across space and time.
