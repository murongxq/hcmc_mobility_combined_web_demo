MOTIVATION

Research Motivation & Core Question

Why do we need a combined reading of distribution, congestion, and public transport supply in Ho Chi Minh City? This section explains the data gap, the modal context, and the project’s core logic before moving into the analytical framework.

Key Motivation

Mobility in Ho Chi Minh City is dominated by motorcycles, while public transport remains limited but potentially critical. However, high-resolution, mode-specific traffic datasets are often unavailable or inconsistent, making it difficult to compare different transport modes fairly across space and time.

We therefore build a workflow that connects street-view object detection, road network structure, and schedule-based public transport supply, and then interpret them under one unified framework.

Why this matters

  • Limited availability of high-resolution, mode-specific traffic data
  • Existing studies often overlook temporal dynamics and modal differences
  • Public transport plays a limited but potentially critical role in mobility restructuring

What This Project Aims to Deliver

The goal is not to claim exact “ground truth” traffic counts, but to build a comparable and interpretable view of mobility dynamics: distribution (where), congestion (how intense), and supply (when and how much).

By placing these dimensions under one shared grid and temporal logic, the project makes it possible to compare demand pressure, road/network support, and public transport provision within the same analytical system.
